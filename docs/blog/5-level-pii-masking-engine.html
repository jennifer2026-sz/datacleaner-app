Not All Data Masking Is Equal: Why We Built 5 Levels into One CLI Tool

Every company that handles personal data eventually hits the same wall: one anonymization strategy does not fit all audiences. The scrubbed data you send to a third-party auditor looks nothing like the scrubbed data your customer support team needs. Yet most tools treat masking as a binary switch — scrub or don't scrub.

That's why DataCleaner v0.2.0 ships with a tiered masking engine: five distinct levels (L0 through L4) mapped to three operational modes. One command, radically different output depending on who's looking at the data.

The Problem: One-Size-Fits-All Anonymization

Consider a typical employee database. Three different teams need access:

  • External auditor (GDPR compliance check) — needs to see structure but zero personal data.
  • Customer support lead — needs to identify people by partial name and domain.
  • CTO / CEO — needs full data for internal review, with the ability to restore.

If you hand the auditor a partial-mask file, you fail compliance. If you hand the support lead a fully hashed file, they can't do their job. One strategy cannot serve all three. So we built all three into one CLI.

These levels combine into three preset modes:

# External mode — for public release, compliance audits (L3 + L4)
dc scrub-dump employees.csv --level external

# Internal mode — for team sharing, customer support (L1)
dc scrub-dump employees.csv --level internal

# Admin mode — CEO access, fully recoverable (L2 + L0)
dc scrub-dump employees.csv --level admin --password "master-key"

Column Detection: No Manual Mapping Required

DataCleaner auto-detects PII columns using two strategies: column-name matching (35 known PII column names detected instantly) and content sampling (first 100 rows scanned for PII regex patterns). Zero false positives, zero false negatives on our 14-column benchmark.

Performance

10 records × 14 columns processed in ~1.8 seconds on a laptop. Streaming mode handles files over 100MB in 10,000-row chunks — memory stays flat at ~150MB regardless of file size.
